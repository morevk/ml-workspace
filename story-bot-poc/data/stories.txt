Once upon a time, there was a little bunny named Fluffy. Fluffy had soft white fur and big floppy ears. Every morning, Fluffy would hop to the garden to eat carrots. The carrots were orange and crunchy. Fluffy loved carrots more than anything in the world. One day, Fluffy met a little bird named Tweety. They became best friends and played together every day. The end.

There was a friendly dragon named Sparky who lived on a green hill. Sparky was small and had shiny blue scales. Unlike other dragons, Sparky did not breathe fire. Instead, Sparky made beautiful rainbow bubbles. All the children in the village loved to play with Sparky. They would chase the colorful bubbles and laugh with joy. Sparky was the happiest dragon in the land.

Little bear Tommy woke up one sunny morning. He stretched his fuzzy arms and yawned. Today was a special day. Tommy was going to learn how to catch fish with his mama. They walked to the river together. The water was cool and clear. Mama bear showed Tommy how to wait patiently. Splash! Tommy caught his first fish. He was so proud and happy.

In a cozy pond lived a green frog named Freddy. Freddy loved to sing songs all day long. Ribbit ribbit, he would sing. The other animals would gather to listen. The ducks would quack along. The fish would blow bubbles. Even the dragonflies would dance. Freddy's songs made everyone smile. Music brings friends together.

A tiny caterpillar named Charlie was very hungry. He crawled along the leaves looking for food. First, he ate one red apple. Then he ate two green pears. Next, he ate three yellow bananas. Charlie grew bigger and bigger. One day, Charlie built a cozy cocoon. When he woke up, he had beautiful wings. Charlie was now a butterfly.

There were three little kittens who lost their mittens. They looked under the bed. No mittens there. They looked in the closet. No mittens there. They looked in the garden. There were the mittens, playing with the butterflies. The kittens were so happy. They put on their mittens and went inside for warm milk and cookies.

Ellie the elephant had a long trunk and big ears. She lived with her family near a watering hole. Every day, Ellie would spray water with her trunk. Whoosh! The water made a rainbow in the sun. Her little brother loved to play in the water. Families are wonderful, thought Ellie. She gave her brother a big elephant hug.

One night, a little star named Twinkle felt lonely in the sky. All the other stars were so far away. Then the moon smiled at Twinkle. Do not be sad, said the moon. You light up the sky for children below. Twinkle looked down and saw a little girl waving. The girl was making a wish on Twinkle. Twinkle felt very special and happy.

A puppy named Max had spots all over his fur. He had one spot on his nose, two spots on his ears, and many spots on his back. Max loved to run and play fetch. His favorite toy was a red ball. When Max was tired, he would curl up on his soft bed. Sweet dreams, little puppy. Tomorrow is another day full of fun.

In the forest lived a wise old owl named Oliver. Oliver had big round eyes and soft feathers. At night, Oliver would hoot softly. Hoo hoo, he would call. The baby animals would listen to his stories. Oliver knew many things about the forest. He taught the young ones to be kind and brave. Knowledge is a wonderful gift to share.

A red ladybug named Lucy had seven black spots. She lived in a beautiful rose garden. Lucy loved to climb up the stems and sit on the petals. From up high, she could see the whole garden. The bees buzzed by to say hello. The butterflies danced in the breeze. Lucy smiled. This garden is my happy home.

There was a little train that thought it could. The train had to climb a big hill. It was hard work. I think I can, I think I can, said the little train. Up, up, up the hill it went. The train did not give up. Finally, it reached the top. I knew I could, said the happy train. Always believe in yourself.

A goldfish named Goldie lived in a round bowl. She had beautiful golden scales that sparkled. Every day, Goldie would swim around and around. She liked to blow bubbles. Pop pop pop went the bubbles. A little boy named Sam would watch and smile. Sam fed Goldie every morning. They were the best of friends.

In a tall tree lived a family of squirrels. Mama squirrel taught her babies how to find acorns. Look under the leaves, she said. The baby squirrels searched and searched. They found many acorns. They stored them for winter. When the snow came, the squirrels stayed warm. They had plenty of food to eat. Planning ahead is very smart.

A little duck named Daisy learned to swim. At first, she was scared of the water. Her mama said, I will be right here with you. Daisy put one foot in the water. It was not so bad. She put the other foot in. Splash! Daisy was swimming. She paddled around the pond. Being brave means trying new things.

Once there was a rainbow that appeared after the rain. It had red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple. A little girl named Emma loved rainbows. She wanted to find where it ended. She walked and walked. But the rainbow stayed far away. Then Emma learned something important. Rainbows are for looking at, not for catching. Beauty is all around us.

A teddy bear named Bruno sat on a shelf. He watched the toys play all day. At night, when everyone was asleep, Bruno would dream. He dreamed of adventures in faraway lands. He dreamed of flying through the clouds. When morning came, Bruno was back on his shelf. But he always had his beautiful dreams.

The sun and the moon were good friends. The sun would shine during the day. The moon would glow at night. They never saw each other. But every morning and evening, they would wave hello. The sun made the sky orange and pink. The moon would appear in the fading light. Friends can be close even when apart.

A baby bird named Pip learned to fly. She stood at the edge of her nest. It looked very high. Her wings were shaking. You can do it, said her mama. Pip jumped and flapped her wings. She was flying! Higher and higher she went. The world looked so big and beautiful. Pip knew she could do anything.

In a small house lived a kind old woman. She baked cookies for all the children. The cookies were shaped like stars and hearts. They smelled like vanilla and cinnamon. Every afternoon, children would visit. They would eat cookies and tell stories. The old woman smiled. Sharing makes everyone happy.
